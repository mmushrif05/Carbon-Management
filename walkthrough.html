Platform Overview

What CarbonIQ does

CarbonIQ is a multi-stakeholder construction carbon accountability platform. It connects clients, consultants, and contractors on a single platform ‚Äî with AI automating data extraction, and structured workflows enforcing accountability at every step.

üîó
Invitation-Only Access
No open registration. Every user is invited by a client or consultant. This ensures the platform only contains verified stakeholders ‚Äî maintaining data integrity across the entire supply chain.
üìä
Role-Based Dashboards
Each of the three roles (Client, Consultant, Contractor) sees a completely different interface tailored to their responsibilities ‚Äî the right data, to the right person, at the right time.
ü§ñ
Three AI Engines
BOQ/Tender Parser extracts quantities from documents. Document Intelligence (RAG) analyses project files with citations. Carbon Advisor recommends targeted reductions per contractor.
‚úÖ
Approval Workflow
Contractors submit data in batches. Consultants review and approve or request edits. Clients see only approved, verified data. Every change creates an audit trail.

Section 05 ‚Äî AI Engine 1

BOQ / Tender Parser

The BOQ Parser is the first AI engine ‚Äî it reads raw tender documents (PDF, Excel, CSV) and automatically extracts material quantities, maps them to carbon emission factors, and builds a carbon forecast ‚Äî all without manual data entry.

‚ö°
The problem it solves
A typical BOQ for a large infrastructure project has hundreds of line items across dozens of pages. Manually extracting, categorising, and mapping quantities to carbon factors takes 3‚Äì6 weeks. The parser does it in seconds ‚Äî enabling carbon decisions at the point of procurement, not after.
1
Document Upload
Consultant uploads a PDF, Excel (.xlsx/.xls), or CSV tender document. The system auto-detects the file type and extracts raw text. PDFs are processed page-by-page using PDF.js. Excel files are converted to structured text via SheetJS.
2
AI Quantity Extraction
The AI reads each BOQ line item and extracts: item description, unit (m¬≤, m¬≥, kg, tonnes, m, nr‚Ä¶), quantity, and thickness/depth where present. Intelligent unit normalisation handles messy AI output ‚Äî detecting and correcting garbage units using pattern matching.
3
Material Matching (Fuzzy ICE DB)
Each extracted item is fuzzy-matched against the ICE Database v3.0 ‚Äî 200+ material types across Concrete, Steel, Asphalt, Aluminum, Glass, Timber, Insulation, MEP, and more. Match confidence scores are calculated and displayed. Low-confidence matches are flagged for review.
4
Smart Unit Conversion
The system handles dimensional conversions automatically. If the BOQ gives m¬≤ but the ICE factor requires m¬≥, the AI extracts the thickness from the description (e.g. "300mm thick concrete slab") and converts. Pipe items detect diameter to select the correct precast factor.
5
Scenario Carbon Forecast
Results are compiled into a named tender scenario with total A1‚ÄìA3 carbon (baseline and target), breakdown by material group, and per-line-item detail. Multiple scenarios can be saved and compared ‚Äî enabling side-by-side evaluation of design alternatives before any procurement commitment.
Section 06 ‚Äî AI Engine 2

Document Intelligence (RAG Pipeline)

The Document Intelligence module allows consultants to upload project documents and run structured AI analysis across five dimensions ‚Äî with every finding traced back to its exact source in the uploaded documents via page-level citations.

üìÑ
Supported document types
Carbon Impact Assessments (CIA), Carbon Emissions Action Plans (CEAP), Technical Reports, Material Submittals, BOQ Specifications, and Sustainability Reports. PDF, Excel, and CSV formats supported.
1
Upload & Chunking
Uploaded documents are extracted to text and split into overlapping semantic chunks. The system auto-detects document type (CIA, CEAP, technical report, material submittal, BOQ spec) and labels each chunk with metadata ‚Äî page number, section heading, detected materials, and topics.
2
RAG Retrieval
When an analysis dimension is run, the system queries the document library using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). The most relevant chunks (up to 25) are retrieved based on semantic relevance to the analysis topic ‚Äî e.g., "material compliance" retrieves specification and material submittal content.
3
Live Project Context Injection
Before sending to the AI, the system builds a live project context from the database: total A1‚ÄìA3 baseline and actual, material-by-material reduction percentages, contractor-by-contractor breakdown, and A5 site totals. This ensures findings are grounded in real project data, not generic.
4
5-Dimension AI Analysis
Each dimension is analysed independently: Material Compliance, Reduction Opportunities, Cross-Document Consistency, Regulatory & Certification Gaps, Actionable Recommendations. Each produces structured findings with severity ratings (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) and explicit recommendations.
5
Page-Level Citation Traceability
Every finding includes citations: document name, page number, section, and a verbatim quote from the source. This means every AI conclusion is verifiable ‚Äî consultants can go directly to the source document and confirm the finding without guessing where the AI got its information.
6
History & Saved Results
Analysis results can be saved to a project history log with timestamp, user name, and finding count. Past analyses are browsable and can be reloaded into the analysis view for comparison. This creates a carbon intelligence audit trail for the project.
Section 07 ‚Äî AI Engine 3

Carbon Advisor

The Carbon Advisor is an AI strategist embedded in the consultant's project view. It reads live project data and generates specific, prioritised reduction recommendations ‚Äî not generic tips, but data-driven actions tied to the actual numbers.

üß†
Key differentiator
The Advisor doesn't just show what happened. It tells the consultant what to do next ‚Äî and specifically which contractor to engage, which material to substitute, and how much carbon reduction that action will deliver.
1
Live Data Payload
The Advisor receives: project metadata, reduction target %, total A1‚ÄìA3 baseline and actual, A4 and A5 totals, material-by-material breakdown with reduction %, and a contractor-by-contractor performance table showing who is above and below target.
2
Targeted Recommendations
The AI analyses which contractors are below target, by how much, and which material categories are driving the gap. It generates role-specific recommendations: for consultants (what to require from contractors), for procurement (material switches and suppliers), and for design (specification changes).
3
Contractor Gap Analysis
For each contractor below target, the Advisor shows exactly how many tCO‚ÇÇ reduction is needed and suggests the specific material substitution or process change that would close the gap. Contractors meeting their target are acknowledged ‚Äî reinforcing good practice.
